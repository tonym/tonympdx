Pop Art Pinup

In 2012 I was doing a lot of catalog and fashion work and wanted to explore classic pinups in the style of the Gibson Girl and early magazine illustrators. So Floofie joined me as stylist and MUAH, and before long, we were working with clothing design and retail company Babygirl Boutique. They have a warehouse at the east end of the Hawthorne Bridge with a photo studio we could use. We paid for the studio by photographing the BGB catalog and styled our photos from the stock on hand. We also had access to a warehouse full of pinup clothing and made the most of it.

Before long, we were publishing a calendar and selling it in local bookstores and at our monthly open studio shows. Then, we moved out of BGB and onto an old loading dock across the street. Here we began taking clients, photographing other fashion designs, and evolving our style. Pinup fashion was and still is a thing, but the mainstream moment had passed. Floofie and I had our own artistic flair driven by whimsy that transcended the project's theme. We published our last calendar in 2015 and moved on to other projects.
